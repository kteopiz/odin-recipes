Overnight, Refrigerator Focaccia = The Best Focaccia Bread Recipe

Description

Focaccia is one of the best beginner breads for its short list of ingredients coupled with a simple and effortless procedure to get your first loaf of homemade bread.

With just your fingers and a 9x13 baking tray, you can have a homemade focaccia loaf made in two days that will give you more than two weeks of goodness (or less if you can't get your hands off it!)

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 2 cups lukewarm water
  • Butter for greasing
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil divided
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons whole rosemary leaves (optional)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl whisk together flour salt and yeast. Add water and mix with a spatula until the liquid is abosrbed and a sticky dough ball has formed. Rub the dough surface lightly with olive oil. Let sit for at least 12 hours up to three days covered and refrigerated
  2. Line a 9x13 baking tray with parchment paper, or grease with butter, or coat with nonstick cooking spray.
  3. Pour the remaining olive oil into the center of your baking tray. and roll in the olive oil to fully cover the dough's surface in oil. Let the dough rest for 3 to 4 hours in the trays
  4. Set a rack in the middle of the oven and preheat to 425 degrees F, if using rosemary sprinkle all over the dough and pour another table spoon of oil over the dough.
  5. Lightly oil your hands and press holes into the surface of the dough and flatten it to spread it across the entire baking tray. Ensure it covers the surface of the pan and has lots of dimples. Sprinkle salt over the now flattened dough
  6. Transfer the pan to the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes until the underside is golden and crisp Once finished transfer the focaccia to a colling rack and let cool for 10 minutes
